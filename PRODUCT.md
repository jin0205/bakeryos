# Product

## Register

product

## Users
BakeryOS is for a commercial sourdough and artisan baker managing daily production, formulas, inventory, costs, sales, and R&D. The user is often working in a real operational context: planning batches, checking ingredient needs, adjusting formulas, reconciling sales, or making decisions quickly between production tasks.

## Product Purpose
BakeryOS exists to make bakery operations clearer, faster, and less error-prone. It should turn formulas, production plans, inventory, costing, and sales data into a practical operating system for the bakery. Success means the user can trust the numbers, find the next action quickly, and move through repeated workflows without friction.

## Brand Personality
Clean, focused, intuitive. The product should feel expert and work-ready without becoming cold or generic. It should have enough warmth and visual character to belong to an artisan bakery, but every design choice must serve operational clarity first.

## Anti-references
Avoid AI UI slop, generic SaaS dashboards, boring color schemes, lazy layout, oversized buttons, excessive empty space, decorative dashboard filler, and designs that feel more like a template than a serious production tool.

## Design Principles
1. Operational density with clarity: show enough information for real work, but structure it so the next action is obvious.
2. Warm precision: keep the bakery character present through restrained warmth, while preserving numerical accuracy and trust.
3. Repeated-use ergonomics: optimize for daily workflows, scanning, comparison, editing, and recovery from mistakes.
4. Interface before decoration: motion, color, cards, and layout must help comprehension or priority, not merely add polish.
5. Trust the craft: formulas, weights, costs, schedules, and inventory states should feel exact, legible, and dependable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No specialized accessibility requirements are currently defined. Maintain strong product defaults: readable contrast, visible keyboard focus, reduced-motion support where motion is added, clear status labels beyond color alone, and layouts that remain usable on common desktop and tablet widths.
